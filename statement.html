Artist Statement

My passion for art wasn't born from a specific epiphany nor date. But I would say it began some time in elementary school with doodling in the margins of papers. With time, those pencil doodles slowly evolved into flowers, faces, and more. While it began creating a line without much thought, as the line continued, the reason behind continuing that line slowly grew- I want to share my story and the passion behind it. Most of my work comprises of pencil, videos, and graphic art, but I have delved into other mediums such as charcoal and sound and still exploring.

While unorthodox, talking to myself really helps in the creative process. Afterall, a thought is simply an unexpressed idea. While talking has its benefits, I may sometimes write down my thoughts or doodle them. Overall, I do not have a true method I strictly follow because I feel that contradicts with the freedom of expression in art. Usually I express my ideas through pencil, not only because it can be used almost anytime and anywhere, but this was the medium that sparked my passion. It stuck with me, through dark and light, thick and thin, literally and figuratively. With videos, in highschool, telling a story or informing someone through thousands of pictures strung together intrigued me, motivating me to create videos. Like video, my interest in graphic art piqued in high school as well. Learning how to use Photoshop and Illustrator greatly expanded my knowledge in how to inform or sway someone with simply images and text. How a font change or the size of an image can greatly impact my message. Like I previously said, while I love using pencil, making videos, and creating graphic art, I am still exploring and trying new ways to share my art.
